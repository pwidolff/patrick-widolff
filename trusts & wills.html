TRUSTS AND WILLS

Trusts & Wills- Drafting a legal will involves creating a document that specifies how your assets and affairs should be managed and distributed upon a person's death. Drafting a legal trust involves creating a document that designates how a person's assets should be managed and distributed by a trustee on behalf of beneficiaries.

In California, both wills and trusts are legal instruments used for estate planning, but they differ in how they function and when they become active. Wills dictate how assets are distributed after death, requiring probate for validation, while trusts can manage assets during life and after, potentially avoiding probate.

Wills:

Function: A will is a legal document that outlines how a person's assets should be distributed after their death.

Probate: Wills require a probate process, which is a court-supervised procedure to validate the will and distribute assets.

Trusts:

Function:

A trust is a legal arrangement where a person (the settlor or trustor) transfers assets to a trustee, who manages them for the benefit of designated beneficiaries.

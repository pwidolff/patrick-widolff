ABOUT US

We are a virtual law firm. We offer better service than traditional law firms. We use up to date technology to make your experience more convenient, with faster results, and more affordable.

I am a sole practitioner attorney. This leads to better results for my clients

Working with a sole practitioner attorney offers several advantages for clients, primarily centered around personalized attention, stronger attorney-client relationships, and potentially lower fees.

With a smaller client base, sole practitioners can offer more individualized attention, enhanced communication, and a greater sense of investment in the client's case.

Personalized Attention and Communication: Sole practitioners often handle all aspects of a client's case, from initial consultation to court representation, ensuring a consistent point of contact and a deeper understanding of the client's needs.

BACKGROUND

I have spent 35 years working at the University of La Verne as a professor in the College of Health. During that time I taught courses in the areas of physiology, biomechanics, general health, etc. Also I served on many college level committees and served as a department chair for a three year period, One of my main duties during my time as a professor was to serve as an advisor to our undergraduate students. My experiences taught me a lot on how to relate to others which has helped me in my relationships with my clients.

In 2013 I decided to attend the La Verne College of Law as part time law student. In 2018 I received my La Verne law degree. I passed the California bar exam in October, 2021. I am registered as a California attorney and my bar # is 338810.

For the last four years I practiced law serving as pro bono immigration attorney. I helped many University of La Verne students with their family’s immigration problems. I also did immigration work for a couple of charity organizations.

While I found legal work in the immigration field rewarding, I am hoping to move on to other areas of law. That is why I started my own law firm where I hope to focus mainly on civil rights and family law.

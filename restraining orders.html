Protective Restraining Orders- Legal directives issued by a court to protect individuals from harassment, abuse, or threats. They can be used in various situations like domestic violence, stalking, and harassment.

In California, protective restraining orders, also known as restraining orders or protective orders, are legal measures designed to protect individuals from harm, harassment, or abuse. These orders can be obtained in various situations, including domestic violence, workplace violence, elder or dependent adult abuse, civil harassment, and gun violence. The specific type of restraining order and the process for obtaining it depend on the nature of the situation and the relationship between the parties involved.

Here's a breakdown of the different types of protective restraining orders in California:

Domestic Violence Restraining Orders:

Purpose: To protect individuals from abuse by a family member, intimate partner, or someone with whom they have a close relationship.

Workplace Violence Restraining Orders:

Purpose: To protect employees from violence or threats of violence in the workplace.

Civil Harassment Restraining Orders:

Purpose: To protect individuals from harassment, threats, or violence by someone they are not in a close relationship with.
